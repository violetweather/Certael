# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Game developers and deployment operators installing or maintaining a self-hosted Certael suite on Windows, Linux, or macOS. They need a guided path when first adopting Certael and complete technical evidence when diagnosing a failed installation.

## Product Purpose

Turn the signed, transactional suite installer into a familiar desktop setup experience without weakening verification, rollback, or auditability. Success means a developer can choose their signed release, review exactly what will be installed, complete or cancel safely, and leave with an actionable result and redacted diagnostic log.

## Positioning

The convenience of an operating-system installer with the same signed inputs, deterministic plan, and recoverable transaction as `certaelctl`.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and transparent. The setup application explains what it is doing, avoids security theater, and keeps advanced evidence available without forcing every user to understand it.

## Anti-references

No marketing-page wizard, neon security visuals, fake progress percentages, hidden downloads, mystery defaults, surprise restarts, glassmorphism, or generic card grids. Do not bury errors behind “something went wrong,” and do not expose secrets in logs.

## Design Principles

- Verify before mutation and make that boundary visible.
- Keep the primary path short while preserving complete redacted diagnostics.
- Show selected components and destination before the operator commits.
- Make cancellation, rollback, retry, and recovery explicit outcomes.
- Use familiar platform controls and keyboard order across every supported OS.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA-equivalent desktop behavior: complete keyboard operation, visible focus, screen-reader names, non-color state communication, 200% scaling, readable contrast, and no time-dependent interaction.
