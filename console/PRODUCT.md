# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary users are game-security and live-operations personnel. They work under time pressure, move between high-volume queues and individual investigations, and need to distinguish explainable abuse from expected player behavior without losing the audit trail.

## Product Purpose

The console turns authoritative gameplay evidence into a focused investigation workflow. Its primary task is to help an operator understand evidence, assemble or review a case, and make a bounded, accountable decision. Success means the operator can reconstruct why a finding exists, act only within granted authority, and leave an immutable record another operator can independently follow.

## Positioning

Certael connects authoritative game events to explainable findings and bounded human decisions without taking ownership of game state, permanent punishment, or appeals.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, forensic. The interface should support sustained concentration and communicate confidence through clarity, not spectacle or alarm.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a neon “SOC wall,” an alarm-heavy red dashboard, a marketing-page layout, glassmorphism, a generic card grid, or a collection of unexplained risk graphics.

## Design Principles

- Evidence before conclusion: show the exact events, rules, versions, fields, and replay context behind every recommendation.
- Calm under pressure: prioritize legibility, predictable structure, and restrained emphasis over visual alarm.
- Decisions stay bounded: make authorization, reason entry, confirmation, and consequences explicit before any restrictive action.
- The audit chain is part of the task: assignments, notes, transitions, and actions must remain reconstructable in context.
- Density must remain navigable: support large result sets and long timelines without hiding meaning behind decorative summaries.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. All investigation and case workflows must be operable by keyboard alone, preserve visible focus, support zoomed text and narrow viewports, and never communicate severity or state by color alone. Reduced motion and high-contrast user preferences must be respected.
